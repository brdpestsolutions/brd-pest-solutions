What We Treat

Pest Control Services in Austin

Pest control built for Austin's hot, humid climate and the unique species pressures of the Texas Hill Country.

General Pest Control

Year-round protection against ants, spiders, roaches, silverfish, and the many other species active in Austin homes.

Scorpion Control

Striped bark scorpions are common across the Austin metro — especially in newer construction and Hill Country homes. We treat cracks, entry points, and harborage areas to keep them out.

Termite Control

Central Texas has significant termite pressure. BRD treats subterranean colonies and protects your home from the structural damage they cause.

Mosquito Control

Austin's warm springs and summers fuel long mosquito seasons. We target breeding sites near standing water so you can enjoy your outdoor spaces.

Fire Ant Treatment

Fire ants are relentless across Central Texas. Our colony-targeted treatments eliminate mounds at the source and prevent rapid re-establishment.

Rodent Control

Roof rats are especially common in Austin's older neighborhoods and near green belts. We locate entry points, eliminate populations, and seal them out permanently.
Know Your Enemy

Austin's Most Common Pest Problems

Austin's warm climate and rapid growth mean pest pressure from all directions. Here's what Central Texas homeowners deal with most.

🦂
Hill Country Threat

Striped Bark Scorpion

Austin's most talked-about pest. They're most active in spring and summer, and especially common in new construction. Their sting is painful — in rare cases dangerous to children and pets.
🐜
Lawn Nuisance

Fire Ant

Fire ants are relentless across Central Texas. They build mounds fast, sting in large numbers, and re-emerge quickly if treatment doesn't reach the queen.
🪵
Structural Risk

Subterranean Termite

Texas has significant termite activity, and Austin is no exception. Underground colonies work silently for years before damage becomes visible.
🦟
Long Season

Mosquito

Austin's warm climate means mosquito season stretches from March through October. The Colorado River and area creeks provide constant breeding habitat.
