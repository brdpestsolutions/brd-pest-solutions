What We Treat

Pest Control Services in Dallas

Pest control built for North Texas's hot summers, active termite zones, and the pest species that thrive in the DFW climate.

General Pest Control

Year-round protection against ants, roaches, spiders, and the dozens of other pest species active in Dallas-area homes.

Termite Control

North Texas has among the highest subterranean termite pressure in the country. BRD's treatment plans protect your structure before damage starts — and eliminate active colonies.

Mosquito Control

DFW summers are long and hot. Mosquito pressure peaks from April through October, and standing water anywhere in the yard is a breeding ground.

Cockroach Elimination

German and American cockroaches are widespread across Dallas. We eliminate infestations at the source and apply residual treatments that stop them from coming back.

Fire Ant Treatment

Fire ants are a constant issue across DFW lawns. Our colony-targeted treatments go beyond the mound to eliminate the queen and prevent re-establishment.

Rodent Control

Roof rats and house mice are active year-round in the DFW area. We identify entry points, remove populations, and seal gaps to prevent re-entry.
Know Your Enemy

Dallas's Most Common Pest Problems

Dallas's heat, humidity, and urban density create intense pest pressure year-round. Here's what DFW homeowners deal with most.

🪵
High Risk

Subterranean Termite

Dallas sits in one of the highest termite-pressure zones in the U.S. Colonies work underground, feeding on structural wood silently for years. Annual inspections are essential.
🪳
Year-Round

German Cockroach

Dallas's heat keeps cockroaches active all year. German cockroaches reproduce rapidly in kitchens and bathrooms — a small infestation becomes a large one in weeks.
🐜
Lawn Pest

Fire Ant

Fire ants are everywhere across North Texas. They move fast, sting repeatedly, and mounds return quickly without colony-targeted treatment.
🦟
Summer Peak

Mosquito

DFW's long hot summers mean mosquito season runs from April through October. Slow-moving water anywhere in the neighborhood sustains breeding populations.
